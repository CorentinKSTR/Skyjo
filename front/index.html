Rules of SKYJO

Objective: The game is played over several rounds. The game ends when a player reaches 100 points or more. The player with the fewest points wins.

Setup: Each player receives 12 face-down cards. One card is placed face-up in the center to start the discard pile. The remaining cards form the draw pile.

Game Play:

  • Each player arranges their 12 cards into four columns of three cards, face down.
  • Each player turns over two of their cards face-up.
  • The player with the highest sum of their two face-up cards starts the game.

On Your Turn:

  • Choose to take the top card from the discard pile or draw a card from the draw pile.
  • If you take a card from the discard pile, replace one of your cards (face-up or face-down) with it.
  • If you draw a card from the draw pile, you can either replace one of your cards with it or discard it and flip one of your face-down cards face-up.

Special Rule: If you get three identical cards in a column, remove those three cards and place them on the discard pile.

End of a Round:

  • When a player reveals all their cards, the round ends after all other players have taken one more turn.
  • All players reveal their remaining face-down cards and calculate their scores.
  • If the player who ended the round does not have the lowest score, their score for that round is doubled.

End of the Game: The game ends when a player reaches 100 points or more. The player with the lowest total score wins.
